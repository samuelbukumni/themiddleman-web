Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026  ·  Effective immediately  ·  Applies to themiddleman.com.ng

1. Introduction

The Middleman ("we", "us", or "our") is a hyperlocal campus marketplace operated by Samuel Oluwabukunmi Oguntona, based at Obafemi Awolowo University (OAU), Ile-Ife, Osun State, Nigeria.

We are committed to protecting your personal data in accordance with the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) 2019, issued by the National Information Technology Development Agency (NITDA), and the Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023.

This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your data. By using our platform or submitting your information, you agree to the practices described here.

2. Information We Collect

We collect the following information when you interact with The Middleman:

  • Full Name — to identify you on the platform
  • Email Address — to send you updates and platform alerts
  • WhatsApp / Phone Number — to facilitate fast communication between users
  • Role Selection — whether you are a Buyer, Seller, or Runner
  • Device & Browser Information — user agent, page visited, referrer URL (for analytics only)
We do not collect your payment card details, BVN, NIN, or any sensitive financial or government identification data at this stage of the platform.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To add you to our early access waitlist and notify you when the platform launches
  • To match you with nearby buyers, sellers, or runners on the platform
  • To send important platform updates, launch announcements, and service communications
  • To improve platform performance and user experience through anonymised analytics
  • To fulfil our legal obligations under Nigerian law

We will never sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

4. Legal Basis for Processing (NDPR Compliance)

Under the NDPR, we process your personal data on the following lawful bases:

  • Consent — you have voluntarily submitted your information to join our waitlist or use our platform
  • Contract — processing is necessary to deliver the service you have requested
  • Legitimate Interest — to operate, secure, and improve our platform in a way that does not override your rights

You may withdraw your consent at any time by contacting us at the details below.

5. Data Storage & Security

Your data is stored securely on Supabase, a cloud database provider. We implement the following security measures:

  • Row Level Security (RLS) — your data cannot be accessed by other users
  • HTTPS encryption — all data transferred to and from our platform is encrypted in transit
  • Access control — only authorised administrators can view submitted data
  • No public API exposure — our database keys are protected and scoped to read-only public operations

While we take every reasonable precaution, no system is 100% secure. We will notify you promptly if a data breach affects your information, in line with NDPR requirements.

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell your data. We may share your information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • With other platform users — only the information necessary to complete a transaction (e.g. your display name and contact when a deal is confirmed)
  • With service providers — such as Supabase (database) and Vercel (hosting), who are bound by their own data protection policies
  • With law enforcement — if required by Nigerian law, a court order, or to protect the safety of users

7. Cookies & Tracking

Our landing page collects basic visit data (page URL, referrer, browser type) for internal analytics. We do not use third-party advertising cookies or tracking pixels.

By continuing to use our website, you consent to this lightweight, privacy-respecting tracking. You can disable JavaScript in your browser to prevent this, though it may affect site functionality.

8. Your Rights Under NDPR

As a data subject under Nigerian law, you have the following rights:

  • Right to Access — request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Right to Rectification — request correction of inaccurate data
  • Right to Erasure — request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to Withdraw Consent — opt out of data processing at any time
  • Right to Object — object to processing based on legitimate interest

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the details in Section 10. We will respond within 30 days.

9. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to provide our services. Specifically:

  • Waitlist data is retained until the platform launches and you create a full account, or until you request deletion
  • Transaction records are retained for a minimum of 2 years for dispute resolution purposes
  • Site visit analytics are retained for 12 months in anonymised form
